## Category B — Multi-Hop

**Q4.** If I'm a Hybrid employee, what financial benefits am I entitled to related to my home setup and ongoing costs, and how do they differ in how they're paid out?

**Expected Answer:**
> As a Hybrid employee, you're entitled to a one-time Home Office Setup Stipend of $750 USD, claimed through Expensify with itemized receipts within 90 days of starting or changing classification. [Paragraph 5] You're also entitled to an ongoing Monthly Connectivity Allowance of $40 USD per month to offset home internet and mobile costs. [Paragraph 6] The key difference is in payment method: the stipend is a one-time reimbursement requiring submitted receipts, while the connectivity allowance is paid automatically through payroll every month with no receipts required. [Paragraph 6]

---

**Q5.** If I'm an Office-Based employee who wants to occasionally work from home, what are my options, and what benefit am I missing out on compared to a Hybrid employee?

**Expected Answer:**
> As an Office-Based employee, you may take up to 4 pre-approved remote work days per calendar month, or more if your department head grants a written exception. [Paragraph 4] Compared to a Hybrid employee, you are missing out on the Monthly Connectivity Allowance of $40 USD per month, since Office-Based employees are explicitly not eligible for that allowance. [Paragraph 6]

---

**Q6.** I want to permanently move to a new country while staying Fully Remote. Do I still need to go through the Global Mobility process, and how does this interact with the approved country list?

**Expected Answer:**
> Yes. Permanent international relocation requires submitting a request through the Global Mobility process at least 60 days in advance, regardless of your current work classification — this applies to Fully Remote employees too. [Paragraph 11] Separately, if the new country is not already on the approved country list (which currently includes the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Ireland, Germany, the Netherlands, Australia, and India), you would also need to submit a Cross-Border Work Request at least 45 days in advance to be permitted to work from that location at all. [Paragraph 2] These are two distinct requirements that both apply in this scenario.
